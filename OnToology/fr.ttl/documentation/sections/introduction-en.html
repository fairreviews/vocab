Introduction back to ToC

The FAIR* Reviews ontology aims at describing scientific reviews as relevant resources produced during the research publication workflow. Reviews are considered to be textual information containing a throughful evaluation of a given scientific contribution. Reviews are written by a reviewer (review author) usually in the context of a convening organization (that might be a scientific conference, a journal, etc.). A review includes the rating given by the reviewer and her/his confidence on the topic when evaluating the contribution.

The FAIR* Reviews ontology is strongly aligned with the SPAR (Semantic Publishing and Referencing) Ontologies.
